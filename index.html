Jose Rizal

Philippine National Hero


Role in the Phillippine Revolution


Contribution to the Phillipines:


  • 1872 - Execution of Filipino patriots known as the Gomburza (GÃ³mez, Burgos, and Zamora), which deeply impacted Rizal and influenced his nationalist views.

  • 1882 - Rizal left for Spain to pursue higher education, marking the beginning of his journey as a reformist and nationalist.

  • 1887 - Publication of "Noli Me Tangere" in Berlin, Germany. The novel exposed the abuses of the Spanish friars and colonial administration in the Philippines, sparking outrage and awakening nationalistic sentiments among Filipinos.

  • 1888 - Rizal established the progressive organization "La Liga Filipina" in Manila, advocating for reforms and national unity among Filipinos.

  • 1892 - Rizal returned to the Philippines from abroad, only to be arrested and exiled to Dapitan in Mindanao under suspicion of inciting rebellion through his writings and activities.

  • 1896 - Despite his advocacy for peaceful reforms, Rizal was implicated in the Katipunan uprising against Spanish rule. He was arrested, tried by a military court, and sentenced to death.

Rizal's contributions to the Philippines are not just confined to his lifetime but have left an enduring legacy that continues to shape the country's national consciousness and identity. His emphasis on education, reform, and peaceful advocacy, combined with his ultimate sacrifice, made him a pivotal figure in the Philippine quest for independence and social justice.
